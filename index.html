About This Visualization

Dataset

This visualization draws on greenhouse gas emissions data compiled by the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) from National Inventory Submissions to the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC). The dataset covers total man-made greenhouse gas emissions — including CO₂, methane, nitrous oxide, and fluorinated gases — expressed in kilograms of CO₂-equivalent per person per year. Land use, land-use change, and forestry (LULUCF) emissions are excluded, following standard reporting conventions. The data spans 2000 to 2023 and was filtered to 41 geographically European countries to focus the narrative on regional trends.

Design Choices

The primary view is a heatmap with countries on the vertical axis and years on the horizontal axis. Rather than encoding raw emissions values directly, each cell encodes the percent change from each country's own 2000 baseline. This transformation was chosen deliberately: it normalizes for the fact that countries begin from very different absolute levels, making it possible to fairly compare progress across the dataset. A country that started at 20 kg/person and dropped to 12 kg is making meaningful progress — a raw value encoding would obscure this relative improvement.

A diverging red-to-green color scale is used, with white/yellow at 0% change. Green encodes reduction (good) and red encodes increase (bad), which aligns with widely understood cultural associations around climate progress. Countries are sorted from top to bottom by their 2023 percent change, so the biggest improvers appear at the top and the biggest backsliders at the bottom — making the overall story immediately readable without requiring the viewer to search.

The secondary view is a line chart that appears when a country is clicked, showing that country's raw emissions trajectory over time alongside a dashed OECD Europe average reference line. This provides important context: the heatmap communicates relative change, while the line chart communicates absolute magnitude and situates the country within a regional benchmark. A vertical annotation marks 2020 to flag the COVID-19 pandemic, which caused a temporary emissions dip across nearly all countries and could otherwise be misread as sustained progress.

Transparency & Accuracy

All data transformations are explicitly disclosed in the subtitle and write-up: the percent change calculation, the 2000 baseline, the exclusion of LULUCF, and the country filter. The data source is cited directly on the chart. Tooltips on both the heatmap and line chart provide exact values on demand, allowing viewers to verify the color encoding against precise numbers. The color scale legend is labeled with its endpoints and midpoint so the encoding is never ambiguous.
